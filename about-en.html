Discover the Project

In a context where legal information is increasingly abundant, complex, and constantly evolving, Dissentis.AI was created with the mission of applying artificial intelligence responsibly and effectively in legal, legislative, and regulatory domains.

Our project aims to simplify access to, understanding of, and analysis of legal knowledge through intelligent tools specifically designed for legal professionals, public institutions, legislative bodies, and corporate legal departments.

Common Challenges When Using AI in Legal Settings

  • Ambiguity and precision of language: Legal texts require exact and contextual interpretation, which many AI systems fail to achieve.
  • Lack of traceability and explainability: Automated decisions from opaque models can generate distrust if not clearly justified.
  • Outdated regulations: Legislation evolves constantly, and many tools do not incorporate real-time updates.
  • Integration difficulties: Most generic AI tools are not designed to interact with legislative databases, case law repositories, or national legal frameworks.
  • Ethical and legal risks: Especially concerning privacy, bias, discrimination, and sensitive personal data usage.

Our Approach

Dissentis.AI addresses these challenges with a specialized approach based on AI models trained on legal data, validated by legal experts, and adapted to national and international regulatory frameworks.

  • We develop intelligent legal assistants capable of understanding legal language, reasoning, and structure.
  • We offer tools for jurisprudence analysis, assisted legal writing, semantic regulation search, and predictive legal risk evaluation.
  • We integrate traceability and explainability systems so every AI-generated result can be audited and justified.
  • We ensure compliance with data protection laws, information access standards, and algorithmic transparency.

At Dissentis.AI, we believe artificial intelligence can transform the legal sector not by replacing professionals, but by enhancing their capacity, precision, and access to legal knowledge. Our commitment is clear: to build ethical, useful, and law-driven technology.
